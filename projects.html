Abenezer Ayana's Projects

  1. BraillEazy

    2. BraillEazy is a business venture I co-founded on November 2020. It is focused on building technologies that help the Blind easily learn and use Braille. Even though Braille is important for the financial and social wellbeing of the blind, Braille literacy is currently at an alarmingly low 10% rate. We saw that the primary reason was the convenience offered by audio technologies like screen readers, and the lack of evolution of Braille. Hence, we designed a smartphone case that allows the blind to read Braille right from the comfort of their palm. We have been involved in multiple entrepreneurial programs and competitions including gALPHA and the Fowler Global Social Innovation Challenge, which have helped us through mentorship and seed funding. BraillEazy is currently completing its incorporation process and aims to apply for research grants that can help us produce prototypes. We have established a partnership with the MN chapter of the National Federation of the Blind, which will help us creating a focus group to test our completed prototypes before we enter production.

  2. Alexa Project - UST School of Law Library

    3. During my year-long work stint at the University of St. Thomas School of Law, I was tasked to create an Amazon Alexa skill that helped law students find available rooms in the Law library. The project required learning about Alexa skills and gaining access of the UST library database. Over the course of the 2018 J-term semester, I completed this project using Alexa Skills Kit and AWS Lambda running on JavaScript. This was my first experience working with University API's that constantly generated security tokens. While the project was not released for University use, it was indicative of the potential voice-assistants have in improving students learning experience.
